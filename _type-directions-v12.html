Pour Over for Raindrop
GitHub Action · Free

Your read-later list, brewed fresh daily.

Connect your Raindrop.io read-later collection and get a beautifully formatted email digest, with optional article recommendations, delivered on your schedule. No dashboard, no app. Just your inbox.

View on GitHub → Fork and set up ↗
01The idea
A read-later list is where good intentions go to quietly die.

You save an article meaning to get to it, and three weeks later it's buried under forty more. Pour Over fixes the part that actually breaks: it brings the queue back to you, on a schedule you set, in a format you'll actually read over coffee.

It runs entirely inside your own GitHub account as a scheduled Action — no server to babysit, no third-party service holding your data, nothing to install on your machine. You point it at a Raindrop collection, pick a cadence, and it does the rest. When you want to change how it behaves, you edit a plain config.yml and commit.

02What it does

Small, sharp, and entirely yours.

Four moving parts, no more than it needs. Everything runs on infrastructure you already have.

01

Recent bookmarks

Pulls the latest saves from any Raindrop collection you choose, respecting tags and filters so the digest stays focused on what you actually meant to read.

02

Smart recommendations

Optional NewsAPI-powered suggestions, drawn from the tags in your archive — so the digest occasionally surfaces something new that fits your taste.

03

Beautiful emails

A clean, typographic digest that reads well on phone and desktop. No tracking pixels, no marketing chrome, no unsubscribe gauntlet. Just the list.

04

Fully automated

Runs on a cron schedule inside GitHub Actions. Set it once and forget it exists until the next digest lands in your inbox at the time you picked.

03How it works

Set up in about ten minutes.

1

Fork the repository

One click puts a copy in your own GitHub account. Everything runs from there — the code, the schedule, and the secrets all stay under your control.

2

Add your keys

Drop your Raindrop token and email credentials into the repository's encrypted secrets. Pour Over never sees them outside your own Actions runner.

3

Pick a schedule

Edit one line of the config to choose your cadence — every morning, weekly on Sundays, whatever fits how you actually read. Commit, and you're done.

04Questions

The practical stuff.

Does it cost anything to run?

No. Public repositories get unlimited GitHub Actions minutes, and a daily digest uses a trivial amount. Raindrop's free tier and most email providers cover the rest. Pour Over itself is free and open source under the MIT license.

Where does my data live?

Entirely in your own accounts. Pour Over reads from Raindrop and sends through your email provider, and the code that ties them together runs in your GitHub Actions runner. There's no Penston Studio server in the middle — I never see your bookmarks or your inbox.

Can I customize the digest?

Yes. The email template is a plain file you can edit, and the config.yml controls cadence, collection, recommendation sources, and how many items each digest includes. If you can read YAML, you can reshape it.

What if I use Gmail or Outlook instead of iCloud?

All supported. Any provider that offers standard SMTP or an app-specific password will work — the setup guide walks through the common ones step by step.

05Works with

Built on Raindrop.io and GitHub Actions, with optional NewsAPI recommendations. Delivers cleanly to iCloud Mail, Gmail, and Outlook — anywhere that speaks standard SMTP. No new accounts, no new subscriptions, nothing to learn beyond a single config file.
